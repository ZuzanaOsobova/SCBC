about

Sid City Book Club has started during the early months of 2020 Covid pandemic, born out of love for reading and the need for structure and community. At first we were reading some of the books recommended and talked about by Sid (Alexander Siddig) during the Sid City Social Club Zoom Meetings, but eventually it the club grew into its own little community. We recommend books, vote on our favourites to read the next month, discuss them during the first Sunday of every month and have a yearly meetup in January to discuss other books we have read and to pick our most and least favourites.

The Book Club was sorta halted and sorta adopted by wider Sid City Social Club during 2021, when Sid started shooting Shantaram. Mel has decided that we all shall read that book and had 5 meetings about it, each discussing one part of the books. The book was... eh let's say it provided us with some interesting discussion and memes. The link to the official SidCity page about Shantaram book club is here, along with some interesting resources put together by the community.

Afterwards the book club continued and is still active (at least as of writing this text, in April 2026). The books we read come from various genres and ideas and backgrounds, but it is truth that we tend to gravitate towards sci-fi and fantasy. Special picks are provided by Sid's upcoming roles or other things relating to him. Sid, if you're reading this, please pick better projects, reading Fallen had us questioning our sanity.

As for this specific book club website, it has been created by Zuzána (she/they), who is a regular at the book club meetings, from the need of having some place to store all the books we have read. As of now, this website is only for storing and showing info about the books, no further functionality, like comments, is programmed. The website has been in creation limbo for a while, with multiple iterations, but in the end, simple HTML+CSS+JavaScript, with no frameworks or libraries was deemed the best way to go. The books are stored in their own little JSON files and then fetched when loading the site, making relatively easy to update. The link to the JSON formatter is here
